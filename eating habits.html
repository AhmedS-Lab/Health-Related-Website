Tips to improve your eating habits

Here are some practical healthy eating tips to help you maintain a balanced diet:

1. Prioritize Whole Foods

Choose whole, unprocessed foods like fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, whole grains, and healthy fats. Limit processed and packaged foods that contain added sugars, unhealthy fats, and preservatives.

2. Eat a Rainbow

Include a variety of colorful fruits and vegetables to get a range of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. Aim for at least 5 servings of fruits and vegetables per day.

3. Balance Your Plate

Fill half your plate with vegetables, a quarter with lean protein (chicken, fish, beans, tofu), and a quarter with whole grains (brown rice, quinoa, whole wheat bread).

4. Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water throughout the day. Aim for at least 8 cups (2 liters) or more if you’re active. Limit sugary drinks like soda and juice, and opt for herbal teas or infused water instead.

5. Control Portion Sizes

Use smaller plates to help with portion control. Avoid eating straight from the bag or container—serve yourself a reasonable portion instead.

6. Eat Mindfully

Slow down and chew your food thoroughly. Avoid distractions like TV or phones while eating to help recognize hunger and fullness cues.

7. Choose Healthy Fats

Opt for sources of healthy fats like avocados, nuts, seeds, olive oil, and fatty fish. Limit trans fats and saturated fats found in fried foods, processed snacks, and fast food.

8. Cut Down on Sugar and Salt

Reduce added sugars by avoiding sweetened beverages, candies, and processed snacks. Limit sodium by choosing fresh foods over packaged ones and using herbs and spices for flavor.
